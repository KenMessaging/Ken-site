It's not just
your messages.

Most apps quietly collect who you talk to, when, where from, and what you do around them. Tied to your phone number, that data is stored, sold, and shared. In the wrong hands, an advertiser, a data broker, or an attacker, it maps your whole life. Ken is built the opposite way. Strong encryption keeps your conversations yours, and we collect nothing worth taking.

What Ken knows about you

Nothing.

You sign up with a username and password. No phone number required, no email, no address book upload. Every message is sealed on your device and only opens on your friend's device through end-to-end encryption, with no one able to decrypt your messages. Not even Ken.

A phone number is optional, only if you choose to add one to find friends who already use Ken. It's never required, and Ken works fully without it.

// everything inside

Built for people who
value privacy.

Privacy is what Ken values. Every feature is built to protect it, from the moment a message leaves your device to the moment it arrives.

Disappearing messages

Set a timer per chat or as your default. Messages vanish from both phones after the time you choose.

Duress PIN

A second PIN that opens a clean decoy account or wipes the app, for when you're forced to unlock.

Spaces

Invite-only groups where members add members. Organize matches, birthdays and dinners with events and RSVPs.

Offline Bluetooth mesh

Reach people nearby with no internet at all. Your message relays through other Ken phones to get there.

Screen lock

Face ID or a PIN to open the app, and previews stay hidden on your lock screen so nothing shows through.

Verify contacts

Compare a safety number to prove there's no one in the middle. Ken warns you if a contact's key ever changes.

Encrypted backup

KenCloud keeps your chats if you switch phones, locked with a key that never leaves your device. We can't read it.

Send money in chat

A built-in wallet to send or request money right inside a conversation, without leaving the app.

Separate identities

Run work and personal accounts side by side on one device, each with its own settings, wallet and lock.

// questions

Answers, in plain terms.

Do I need a phone number to use Ken?

No. You create an account with just a username and password. You can add a phone number later if you want to find friends who use Ken, but it is never required.

How does messaging work without internet?

Ken uses a Bluetooth mesh. Your encrypted message is relayed from device to device through other people running Ken nearby, until it reaches the recipient. No cell tower or wifi is needed.

What data does Ken collect about me?

As little as possible. No message content, no contact lists, and no location. Messages can only be read on your device, so there is no profile of you to build, share, or lose.

Is Ken really free?

Yes. Ken is a non-profit funded by donations. There are no ads, no subscriptions, and no selling of your data.

How far can a message travel over Bluetooth?

There is no fixed limit. Every device running Ken passes the message along, so as long as there are people relaying it, a message can travel across a crowd, a neighborhood, or further.
